Sabah's Socio-economic Strain

Leading in Both Poverty and Unemployment


Sabah struggles with both high poverty and unemployment due to limited economic opportunities, especially in rural areas, compounded by infrastructure challenges. This has created a cycle where job shortages lead to increased poverty and social strain.

In W.P. Labuan, high unemployment persists, but poverty is more controlled, likely due to federal support and smaller population size, which helps buffer the economic impact despite limited job prospects.

Malaysia's economic center is located in the Central Region, therefore Selangor, W.P. Putrajaya & W.P. Kuala Lumpur benefits from more diverse job opportunities, resulting in low poverty and moderate unemployment, though urban living costs can still strain some households financially.
